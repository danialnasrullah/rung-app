# Rung — House Rulebook (Closed Variant)

Living document. Update this file as house rules are added or amended.

## 1. Overview

Rung (aka Rang/Court Piece/Hokm) is a 4-player, 2-team trick-taking card game played with a standard 52-card deck. This rulebook covers the **Closed Rung** variant, which we are building first. Open Rung (where the trump suit is announced aloud) is a future variant, not covered here.

## 2. Players & Teams

- 4 players, 2 fixed partnerships, partners sit crosswise (opposite each other).
- One player deals; the player to the dealer's side becomes the **rung selector** for that hand.
- **Pre-match draw:** before the match starts, players draw a card each; the player with the lowest card deals the first hand.
- **Dealer rotation:** after each hand, the **losing team** deals the next hand.

## 3. Dealing

- Cards are dealt in batches of **5, 4, 4** (5 cards first, then 4, then 4).
- The rung selector looks at their first 5 cards and picks the trump suit (the "rung") **without announcing it** — this is what makes it the Closed variant.
- Remaining cards (4 + 4) are dealt after the rung is selected.

## 4. Trump Selection (Closed Rung)

- The rung selector chooses the trump suit privately based on their first 5 cards. Other players learn the trump suit only through play (e.g., seeing it used to cut).

## 5. Play

- One full round of 4 cards (one per player) is called a **sar**.
- A hand consists of 13 sar total.
- Standard trick-taking rules apply: players must follow the suit led if able.
- The player who plays the highest card of the suit led — or the highest rung (trump) card if any rung was played — is **senior** for that sar.
- The senior player of a sar leads the next sar.
- There are no point values assigned to individual cards.

## 6. Cutting

- **Cutting** = playing a rung (trump) card when you cannot follow the suit led, in order to become senior for that sar.
- A player may only cut if they do not hold the suit currently led.
- **Cut hierarchy:** a cut becomes the absolute senior for that sar — it beats any non-rung card regardless of the suit led — unless a later player also cuts with a **higher rung card**, in which case that higher cut becomes senior instead. Whoever plays the highest rung card among all cuts in the sar wins it.

### 6.1 Forced cut rule

- If the rung-selecting team is in a position to **pick** the current sar (i.e., they would complete 2 consecutive senior wins and claim the heap), the **last player to act in that sar** — who is on the opposing team — **must cut if able to**, in order to prevent the pick.

## 7. Picking a Sar (claiming the heap)

- Sar are not claimed immediately when won. They accumulate in a heap.
- To **pick** the heap, the **same player** must be senior on **2 consecutive sar**.
- The heap cannot be picked before the 5th sar. The 5th sar is the earliest sar that can be picked.
- **Ace exception:** a player can be senior on a sar by winning it with an Ace, but a sar won via Ace **does not count toward completing a pick**. In other words, being senior with an Ace breaks/doesn't advance the 2-consecutive streak needed to pick, even though the player is still recognized as senior for that sar.

## 8. Winning the Hand

- The **rung-selecting team** wins the hand only by picking **all 13 sar** (a full sweep — equivalent to a kot).
- The **non-rung team** wins the hand by picking just **2 sar** — meaning one successful pick (2 consecutive senior wins by the same player).
- There is no other scoring system (no match points, no running tally) beyond this per-hand win/loss for now.

## 9. Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Rung | The trump suit for the hand |
| Rung selector | Player who privately chooses the rung after seeing their first 5 cards |
| Sar | One round of 4 cards (one trick) |
| Senior | The player who wins a given sar |
| Pick | Claiming the heap of accumulated sar, requires 2 consecutive senior wins by the same player |
| Cut | Playing a rung card to win a sar when unable to follow suit |
| Forced cut | Mandatory cut by the last player of a sar to block the rung team from picking |
